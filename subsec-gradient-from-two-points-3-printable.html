    \(\textbf{Work in Pairs}\)
    🔗
    What you need:
    🔗
    • Graph paper or squared notebook
      🔗
    • Ruler
      🔗
    • Pencil
      🔗
    • Calculator
      🔗
    🔗
    \(\textbf{Instructions}\)
    🔗
    1. On a piece of graph paper, plot the following two points: \(A(2, 3)\) and \(B(6, 7)\text{.}\)
      🔗
    2. Draw a straight line passing through both points using a ruler.
      🔗
    3. Count how many units up (rise) and how many units across (run) from point A to point B.
      🔗
    4. Record your values and use the gradient formula to find the slope between the two points.
      🔗
    🔗
    Discuss: What does the gradient tell you about this line?
